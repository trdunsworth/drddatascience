I started with working with foundational models to see if they are as good or better on highly stochastic datasets like inbound volumes. I’ve seen some promise that way. I am not a fan of Erlang models since the stochasticity of the data seems to make the algorithm inaccurate. I’ve recently been reading a book from a professor who has been investigating the intersection of queuing and game theories. If you can model the behaviour entering the queue and accurately describe the server pool and access points, then the algorithm can fall into place with forecast volumes and translate that into FTEs at a required service level.
